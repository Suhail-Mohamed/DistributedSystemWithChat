Course Format and Roadmap

One topic per lesson. Each lesson teaches the background, then asks you to experiment, answer questions, and submit a design artifact for review.

Lesson shape

1. What you should know

Definitions, mental model, and the details that usually trip people up.

2. Try the technology

A small experiment using a real tool: DuckDB, Postgres, Docker, Redis, or a stream system.

3. Answer questions

Prediction questions, observation questions, and design questions.

4. Submit

Use the HTML answer boxes, export your submission, and paste it into chat for review.

Review rubric

AreaWhat I will check
Access patternsDid the design start with reads/writes instead of technology names?
Data modelDoes the schema/key model support the product behavior?
Indexes and plansDo the indexes match the query shapes?
Partitioning/shardingDoes the placement strategy avoid obvious hotspots?
CorrectnessAre concurrency, retries, idempotency, and consistency handled?
Cloud realismDoes the design mention managed services, regions/AZs, cost, and observability?
Failure modesDoes the design admit what breaks and how recovery works?

Submission rule

For each lesson, the deliverable is not a polished essay. It should be your best current explanation plus experiment results. Submit even if uncertain; the review is where we tighten the details.
